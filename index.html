2. Why anything, why now, why Glean

Why anything – a Work AI fabric for Flex

Falcon Flex is already a >$1.35B ARR engine growing >200% YoY, with the goal to make it the standard licensing model while continuing to expand operating margins. The current model—hiring more people and relying on heroic individual effort to manage complexity— does not scale.

To avoid hitting an execution ceiling, CrowdStrike needs to deliver a true enterprise AI experience that:

  • Gives every employee a unified way to find, understand, and act on information.
  • Translates directly into measurable productivity gains and higher employee satisfaction.

Why now – this planning cycle

Flex has clear product–market fit and is re-accelerating net-new ARR, but GTM and operations today rely on:

  • Tribal knowledge scattered across tools and teams.
  • “Hero” efforts to build ad-hoc internal tools and workflows.

As more Flex modules come online and CrowdStrike scales globally, this model will not sustain Flex growth ambitions. Now is the window to:

  • Standardize and codify what’s working before complexity outpaces process.
  • Put a central AI layer in place that elevates every employee’s day-to-day work while Flex scales.

Why Glean – strategic platform choice

  • Enterprise-ready foundation: Connects securely to 100+ systems (Falcon Flex data, collaboration, CRM, ITSM, Dev tools, HR, etc.) while honoring existing security and permissions.
  • Single Work AI fabric: A unified system of context for every employee: search, assistant, and agents in one place.
  • Builder platform: Allows CrowdStrike teams to leverage advanced MCP and agent capabilities rather than reinventing core AI plumbing.

Based on the joint ROI model, this partnership is expected to unlock tens of millions of dollars in annual productivity savings that can be reinvested into higher-value work, including Falcon Flex strategy and execution.

4. Alternative plan – Self Key (reference only)

4.1 Self Key overview

  • Product: Glean EnterpriseFlex.
  • Hosting: Self-hosted in CrowdStrike’s environment.
  • LLM key model: Self Key (CrowdStrike-provided LLM key).
  • Users: Same 12,000-user end-state, but with separate economics and responsibilities.
  • CrowdStrike owns: LLM procurement, provisioning, capacity planning, routing, fallbacks, and LLM incident response.

4.2 Trade-offs vs. Glean Key

  • Pros: More control over underlying LLM vendor and tenancy; discounted seat and credit economics to reflect infra costs borne by CrowdStrike.
  • Cons: More internal operational work (capacity, reliability, upgrades, troubleshooting); some advanced features may lag or require extra alignment.

Side-by-side view

  • A
    Glean Key (recommended)
    Glean manages the universal key, capacity, and failover. Faster access to new models, Deep Research, and advanced agents with simpler seat + credit economics and lower infrastructure overhead.
  • B
    Self Key
    CrowdStrike manages LLM contracts and runtime. TCO is more variable and requires deeper long-term ownership by infra and security teams, even if per-seat pricing is lower.

The proposal keeps Self Key as a viable alternative, but the primary recommendation and ROI modeling focus on Glean Key, where value realization is highest and complexity lowest.

6. Implementation & joint operating model

6.1 Deployment approach

  • Self-hosted + Glean Key in CrowdStrike’s AWS environment.
  • Joint architecture and security review, leveraging the work completed during the pilot.
  • Phased rollout to:
    • Falcon Flex core teams and supporting functions.
    • Broader enterprise cohorts as value and adoption are proven.

6.2 Outcomes program

The Outcomes package is explicitly tied to:

  • Realizing the search & assistant productivity assumptions in the ROI model.
  • Defining and tracking Flex-specific KPIs (e.g., time-to-answer, time-to-first-draft, number of Flex deals supported).
  • Enabling GTM agent use cases (Account 360, proposals/RFPs, deal strategist, competitive intel) once the foundation is stable.

Joint cadence & governance

  • 1
    Executive alignment
    Confirm Falcon Flex objectives and the small set of metrics that will serve as the joint scorecard.
  • 2
    Technical deep-dive
    Finalize self-hosted architecture, security posture, and data-source coverage using pilot learnings.
  • 3
    Wave-based rollout
    Begin with Flex-critical teams, then expand toward the full 12,000-user footprint as value is proven.
  • 4
    Value realization & expansion
    Track ROI realization vs. model, then layer in additional use cases to compound value over time.

7. Commercial summary (Glean Key)

  • Product & deployment: Glean EnterpriseFlex, self-hosted + Glean Key.
  • Users: Path and price protection to 12,000 licensed users.
  • Economics: Custom CrowdStrike-only pricing including:
    • EnterpriseFlex licensing.
    • Premium Support.
    • Optional Protect+.
    • Outcomes package.

In Year 1, CrowdStrike’s commercial commitment is for 2,800 users, but if adoption exceeds expectations, Glean will allow deployment up to 6,000 users without additional Year-1 license fees. Any true-up to 12,000 users applies only to Year 2 and beyond.

This structure:

  • Demonstrates goodwill by maximizing what can be deployed in Year 1 within your budget.
  • Provides clear line-of-sight and price protection on scaling to 12,000 users.
  • Gives Glean the long-term commitment needed to justify pricing and upfront investment in your success.
